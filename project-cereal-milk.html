Cereal Milk Initiative

A STEM education initiative that teaches elementary and middle school kids through creative problem-solving — then turns them into student-teachers, creating a self-sustaining learning community.

Type
Founded Initiative
Founded
2025
Partner
Local Library
Scope
Elementary–Middle School

The Idea

The Cereal Milk Initiative started from a simple belief: kids learn best when they're given real problems, creative latitude, and the responsibility of teaching someone else.

Named to evoke something everyday and nourishing, the initiative runs STEM classes at a partnered local library, targeting elementary to middle school students in Charlotte, NC.

Three Pillars

  • Learning with creative problem-solving — classes are hands-on and open-ended, not lecture-based
  • Learning by teaching others — after attending a class, students become 'student teachers' for the next cohort
  • Building a learning community — the student-teacher model creates a domino effect, expanding reach without expanding cost

Impact

Multiplier Effect

By training students to become teachers, each cohort multiplies the initiative's reach. A student who attends 3 sessions and then teaches 5 others has already 6× the original investment.
