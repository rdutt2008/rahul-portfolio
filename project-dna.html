DNA Sequence Detection

An AI model that takes strands of DNA sequences as input, splices them, and detects start and stop codons — helping biologists segment large genomic strands and identify disease mechanisms.

Type
University Research — Prof. Donald Jacobs
Dept
Physics, UNC Charlotte
Stack
TensorFlow, NumPy, Python
Status
In Development

Research Context

This project is part of ongoing research led by Professor Donald Jacobs in the Department of Physics at UNC Charlotte. The goal is to give biologists a computational tool for dissecting large DNA sequences into functionally meaningful segments.

The Problem

DNA sequences in raw form are enormous — identifying where functional genes start and stop within a large strand requires finding start codons (ATG) and stop codons (TAA, TAG, TGA). Doing this manually or with rule-based tools misses context and doesn't scale.

Our Approach

  • Input: raw DNA sequence strings of variable length
  • Preprocessing: sliding window tokenization, codon encoding
  • Model: sequence-based neural network trained to predict codon boundaries
  • Output: annotated strand with start/stop codon positions flagged
  • Enables splitting large strands into smaller, biologically meaningful segments

Scientific Impact

Why it matters

Identifying start/stop codon sites in large DNA strands increases understanding of functional vs. dysfunctional DNA, directly improving the efficiency of identifying disease mechanisms.
