Simulation of Peptide Interactions

A Python model that simulates polar-polar, polar-nonpolar, and nonpolar-nonpolar molecular interactions across thermodynamic variables. Part of a novel library used by physicists at UNC Charlotte.

Overview

Proteins fold and interact based on the thermodynamic properties of their constituent amino acids. Simulating these interactions — even at a simplified level — gives physicists insight into how molecules behave under varying temperature and energy conditions.

This model was built as a component of a larger Python library being developed in Professor Jacobs' lab, allowing researchers to plug in thermodynamic values and visualize molecular behavior.

Model Inputs

  • Free energy (G) — drives the spontaneity of molecular interaction
  • Enthalpy (H) — heat content of the molecular system
  • Entropy (S) — disorder and randomness in the system
  • Heat capacity (Cp) — temperature sensitivity of enthalpy

Simulated Interaction Types

  • Polar–Polar: hydrogen bonding and electrostatic attraction
  • Polar–Nonpolar: hydrophobic effect and entropic penalties
  • Nonpolar–Nonpolar: van der Waals forces and hydrophobic clustering
  • Thermodynamic curves plotted across iterating temperature ranges

Library Integration

Contribution

This model was incorporated into a novel Python library alongside other existing models, enabling physicists to plot molecular interaction landscapes based on user-defined thermodynamic parameters.
